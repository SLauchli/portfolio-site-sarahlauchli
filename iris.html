Case Study

UX/UI Designer Sarah Lauchli

10/26/20 - 11/26/20

App Context

Iris is a native e-commerce app for purchasing eyeglasses and was created during my UI Design course at CareerFoundry. The brief I was given described an online shopping experience that would be easy to use and customize for the user.

Objective

Using 3D scanning, give customers the ability to see what they look like wearing various frames, and provide smart filters for easier search options within the app.

Challenge

Glasses are expensive and if they are ordered (in person or online) they take a week or two to arrive. Trying on glasses is difficult because you can’t see how you look through prescription-free frames and you don’t know how these glasses are going to feel on your face in a few hours.

Truth

Technology has changed and we now have access to new solutions that are waiting to be applied to old ways of doing things. The Vision Council of America says 64% of Americans wear corrective eyewear and 85% wear sunglasses.

Action

Iris makes use of iOS’s built-in 3D scanning technology to scan customers’ faces and identify face measurements to prevent customers from ordering glasses that don’t fit. The 3D face scan gives customers a chance to try on as many frames as they want and accurately see how they would look in each pair.

01 | Understand

Project Brief

The brief described an online shopping experience that would be easy to use and customize for the user.

Selecting the user flows that would showcase the app’s advanced filtering, I focused on an app that could be more customizable than a website to create more value for the theoretical company.

Market Research

Many eyewear sites allow customers to upload an image of themselves so they can see how various frames might look. These images lack facial measurements making them misleading.

Some sites provided tedious measurement explanations most users won’t go through if they’re in a hurry. It creates extra steps that make even this solution a chore—especially if measurements aren’t taken correctly. Glasses would need to be returned and the process re-started.
